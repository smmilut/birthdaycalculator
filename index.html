Birthday Calculator

If you are born on February 29th, when are your birthdays ?

Calendars are convenient approximations for the two main movements of our planet Earth : rotation around its own axis (what we call "days"), and revolution around the Sun (what we call "years").

In nature, those two movements happen completely independently. There is no way a year can be made of an exact number of days.

As a consequence, we can choose two different definitions for "birthdays" :

  1. Celebrate when the calendar happens to display the same date numbers for day and month. If you are not born on February 29th, this is convenient and approximately correct.
  2. Celebrate when the Earth has made an exact number of revolutions around the Sun. This is harder to calculate, and you can find the results on this page.

A funny consequence of calculating birthdays with the second method : your birthday will sometimes happen on a different calendar day. This time for everybody, not just the unlucky February 29th people.

To understand, remember that on leap years the calendar year is suddenly 1 day longer. So when you celebrate using the calendar (method 1.) you are actually 1 year + 1 day old, oops wrong day for the party !
